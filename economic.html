Economic Sustainability: SOC Analyst

Findings

The SOC analyst career path is one of the most critical and fastest-growing specializations in cybersecurity. As organizations face an estimated $8 trillion in annual cybercrime costs, SOC analysts serve as the frontline defenders of our increasingly connected world.

According to the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, information security analyst roles—including SOC positions—are projected to grow by 32% between 2022 and 2032, nearly ten times faster than the average for all occupations. In 2024 alone, more than 30,000 SOC analyst roles were open globally, highlighting strong and sustained demand and creating significant opportunities for entry and advancement.

Compensation in this field reflects both demand and responsibility. Entry-level SOC analysts in the United States typically earn between $60,000 and $90,000 annually. Mid-level professionals with two to five years of experience often command salaries ranging from $90,000 to $120,000, frequently taking on incident response leadership and developing specialized technical expertise. Senior SOC analysts and team leads can earn $120,000 to $160,000 or more, with roles that require deep experience, advanced certifications, and proven leadership skills.

Beyond base salary, SOC analyst roles often include comprehensive benefits packages, such as health insurance, retirement contributions, professional development support, and flexible work arrangements—further enhancing overall compensation.

Students who choose cybersecurity as their major gain access to a wide range of career opportunities across diverse sectors, including finance, healthcare, government and defense, technology and IT services, and education.

While there is strong demand for SOC analysts and a significant number of open positions, cybersecurity is generally not considered an entry-level field. Even junior roles require a solid foundation of technical skills, practical experience, and a clear understanding of security principles. As a result, candidates are expected to demonstrate relevant competencies to be competitive, even for entry-level SOC analyst positions.
